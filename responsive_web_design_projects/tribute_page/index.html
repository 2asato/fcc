Ken Griffey Jr.

"The Kid"

Ken Griffey Jr, owner of the sweetest swing and follow through in baseball.

    TIMELINE OF KEN GRIFFEY JR'S CAREER

  • 1987 - Seattle Mariners select Ken Griffey Jr. with the No. 1 pick in the draft.
  • 1989 - At age 19, Griffey doubles off Dave Stewart in his first major-league at-bat.
  • 1989 - On the first pitch he saw at the Kingdome, Griffey hits a home run.
  • 1990 - Griffey is the first Mariner elected as a starter for the All-Star Game, the first of 11 straight years he was selected for the game.
  • 1990 - Hits back-to-back home runs with his father, Ken Griffey Sr.
  • 1990 - Wins the first of 10 consecutive Gold Gloves.
  • 1992 - All-Star Game MVP after going 3 for 3, including a home run off Greg Maddux. It is the first home run by a Mariner in an All-Star Game.
  • 1993 - Ties major-league record by hitting home runs in eight straight games.
  • 1994 - Griffey wins the first of three Home Run Derby titles.
  • 1995 - Scores on The Double. You may have heard of this.
  • 1997 - Griffey is the 13th player to be named unanimous league MVP after hitting .304 with 56 home runs and 147 RBI.
  • 1999 - Griffey is named to the 30-player All-Century team, and is named Player of the Decade.
  • 2000 - The Mariners send him to Cincinnati for outfielder Mike Cameron, pitcher Brett Tomko, infielder Antonio Perez and minor-league pitcher Jake Meyer.
  • 2008 - Traded by the Reds to the White Sox for pitcher Nick Masset and infielder Danny Richar.
  • 2009 - Griffey chooses the Mariners over Atlanta, signing a one-year deal with the Mariners.
  • 2010 - Griffey retires with career numbers of .284 BA, 630 HR, 1836 RBI
